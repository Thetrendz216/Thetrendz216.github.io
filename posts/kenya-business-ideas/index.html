Top 10 Business Ideas in Kenya for 2026

April 20, 2026 · 2 min

Introduction#

Kenya has one of the most vibrant and entrepreneurial economies in Africa. With a young population, growing middle class and excellent mobile money infrastructure, 2026 is the perfect time to start a business in Kenya.

1. Mobile Money Agency#

M-Pesa agents are still in high demand across Kenya especially in rural areas. Start up costs are low and you earn commission on every transaction. A busy agent can earn KSH 30,000-100,000 per month.

2. Online Delivery Service#

E-commerce is booming in Kenya. Start a local delivery service connecting online shoppers with their packages. Partner with Jumia, Glovo or start your own delivery business.

3. Digital Marketing Agency#

Most Kenyan businesses need help with social media and online marketing. Learn digital marketing skills and offer services to local businesses. Charge KSH 10,000-50,000 per month per client.

4. Agribusiness and Farming#

Agriculture is the backbone of Kenya. Profitable farming ventures include:

  • Poultry farming (chicken and eggs)
  • Greenhouse vegetable farming
  • Mushroom growing
  • Rabbit keeping

5. Boda Boda Business#

Motorcycle taxis remain extremely popular across Kenya. Buy one or two motorcycles and hire riders. Each boda earns KSH 800-1500 per day.

6. Beauty and Hair Salon#

Beauty services are always in demand. A well located salon in a busy area can earn KSH 50,000-200,000 per month. Specialize in natural hair care or braiding to stand out.

7. School Catering#

Supply food to schools and colleges. With thousands of students needing daily meals this is a consistent and profitable business with guaranteed customers.

8. Real Estate Agency#

Help people buy sell and rent properties in your area. Real estate agents earn 3-5% commission on every property deal. One sale can earn KSH 50,000-500,000!

9. Solar Energy Solutions#

Kenya has abundant sunshine making solar a great business. Sell and install solar panels for homes and businesses. Government incentives make this even more profitable in 2026.

10. Event Planning#

Kenyans love celebrations! Start an event planning business for weddings, corporate events and graduations. Event planners earn KSH 20,000-200,000 per event.

Conclusion#

Kenya offers incredible opportunities for motivated entrepreneurs. Pick one business idea that matches your skills and budget, start small and grow consistently. The Kenyan economy rewards those who take action and never give up!
