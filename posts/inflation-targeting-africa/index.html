Inflation Targeting in Africa: How Central Banks Are Fighting Price Rises

April 23, 2026 · 3 min · The Trendz

Introduction#

African central banks are employing increasingly sophisticated inflation targeting frameworks in 2026 to manage price pressures and maintain currency stability in ways that previous generations of African monetary authorities could not achieve. The results are mixed with some countries achieving genuine success in bringing inflation down to sustainable levels while others continue struggling with persistent inflationary pressures.

Understanding how central banks target inflation and what this means for interest rates currency values and investment returns is essential knowledge for African investors and savers navigating the financial landscape.

How Inflation Targeting Works#

Central banks following inflation targeting frameworks set a specific numerical inflation target typically 2-4% and use their policy tools primarily short term interest rates to keep actual inflation close to the target.

When inflation rises above the target the central bank raises interest rates to cool economic activity reduce demand and bring inflation back down. When inflation falls below the target the central bank reduces interest rates to stimulate economic activity and push inflation back up toward the target.

The framework depends on central bank credibility. If markets and businesses believe that the central bank is committed to its inflation target and has the tools to achieve it inflation expectations remain anchored and inflation actually becomes easier to control. If credibility is low inflation expectations become unanchored and inflation is much harder to bring down.

African Central Banks Using Inflation Targeting#

South Africa’s Reserve Bank has the longest history of inflation targeting in Africa having adopted the framework in 2000. The SARB has achieved a reasonable degree of success maintaining inflation within its 3-6% target band most of the time though commodity price shocks and currency movements still create periodic challenges.

Kenya’s Central Bank has been practicing inflation targeting since 2006 and has improved its framework significantly particularly in recent years. Recent decisions including multiple interest rate increases in 2023-2024 demonstrate the CBK’s commitment to fighting inflation even at the cost of slower economic growth.

Nigeria’s Central Bank has employed inflation targeting more irregularly with periods of credible commitment interrupted by political pressure for lower rates to support government spending. The credibility challenges mean that inflation targeting has been less effective in Nigeria than in South Africa.

Ghana’s Central Bank adopted formal inflation targeting in 2015 and has pursued it with genuine commitment achieving significant inflation reduction from double digit levels to near single digits.

The Trade Offs#

Central banks face genuine trade offs when pursuing inflation targeting. Higher interest rates reduce inflation but also slow economic growth reduce employment and increase the cost of borrowing for businesses and government.

The optimal inflation target balances controlling inflation against maintaining economic growth and employment. Most central banks have concluded that the benefits of stable low inflation outweigh the costs of the short term economic slowdown required to achieve it.

Implications for Savers and Investors#

Inflation targeting affects savers and investors through its influence on interest rates and currency values. Higher interest rates to fight inflation improve savings returns and fixed income investment returns but reduce stock market valuations. Lower inflation reduces the purchasing power erosion that savers experience over time.

The most beneficial scenario for savers and investors is successfully controlled inflation achieved through credible central bank action that keeps interest rates reasonable. This scenario is most likely in countries with credible central banks strong institutional frameworks and political commitment to price stability.

Conclusion#

Inflation targeting represents a significant improvement in monetary policy framework relative to previous approaches that relied on discretion and were often subordinated to political pressure. African countries with credible inflation targeting frameworks and committed central banks achieve better outcomes for currency stability investment returns and purchasing power preservation than those without.
