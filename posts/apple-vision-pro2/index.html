Apple Vision Pro 2 Review: Is It Worth Buying in Africa 2026?

April 22, 2026 · 3 min

Introduction#

Apple has released the Vision Pro 2 at a significantly reduced price point of $1,999 making spatial computing more accessible than ever before. The second generation device addresses many of the criticisms of the original while delivering dramatically improved performance comfort and battery life that make it a genuinely compelling product rather than an expensive experiment.

For Africans considering whether this device is worth the investment the answer depends significantly on your professional context and how you intend to use it. This comprehensive review covers everything you need to know about the Vision Pro 2 and whether it makes sense for African users.

What’s New in Vision Pro 2#

The weight reduction from 600 grams in the original to just 280 grams in the Vision Pro 2 is perhaps the most practically impactful improvement. The original was uncomfortable for extended use due to its weight. The new model can be worn comfortably for several hours making it practical for actual productive work rather than just short demonstrations.

Battery life has doubled to 4 hours of continuous use with the external battery connected and 2 hours standalone. While not yet sufficient for a full work day this represents meaningful improvement toward practical daily use.

The display has been upgraded to achieve 4K per eye resolution making text and fine details significantly sharper than the already impressive original. This improvement is particularly noticeable for productivity use cases involving reading documents and spreadsheets.

Processing power from the new M4 chip makes the Vision Pro 2 significantly faster than its predecessor with AI powered features that enable more natural interaction through improved eye tracking hand gesture recognition and voice commands.

Practical Use Cases for African Professionals#

Remote collaboration is the most compelling professional use case for the Vision Pro 2. The ability to have virtual meetings where remote participants appear as life size spatial presences rather than small video thumbnails transforms the experience of remote work in ways that 2D video calls cannot.

For African professionals who work with international clients or colleagues this spatial meeting capability creates a sense of presence and connection that reduces the friction of remote collaboration significantly.

Architecture engineering and design professionals can use the Vision Pro 2’s spatial computing capabilities to review 3D models at full scale walk through architectural designs virtually and collaborate on spatial projects in ways that traditional screens cannot enable.

Medical and healthcare professionals are finding applications in surgical planning medical training and remote specialist consultation that leverage the device’s precise spatial display capabilities.

Should African Consumers Buy Vision Pro 2?#

The honest answer for most African consumers is not yet. At $1,999 the Vision Pro 2 remains an expensive device whose most compelling capabilities require infrastructure including fast reliable internet and compatible software ecosystems that are not yet fully developed across most of Africa.

For high income professionals in specific fields including architecture medical education design and remote collaboration with international partners the productivity gains may justify the investment today. For most others waiting for the third generation device which will likely bring further price reductions and capability improvements is the more financially sensible choice.

Conclusion#

The Apple Vision Pro 2 is a genuine technological achievement that represents the direction of computing interfaces. Its practical applications for work creativity and collaboration are real and growing. The price while significantly reduced from the original remains high for most African consumers. Watch this space as the technology matures and prices continue declining toward the mass market inflection point.
