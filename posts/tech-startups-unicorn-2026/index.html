Tech Startups on Track to Become Africa's Next Unicorns in 2026

April 23, 2026 · 2 min

Introduction#

Several African technology startups are on trajectory to become the continent’s next unicorn companies with valuations approaching or exceeding $1 billion. These companies span multiple sectors from logistics to education to healthcare and represent the maturation of African entrepreneurship beyond the fintech focus that dominated earlier unicorn creation.

Understanding which companies are on the verge of unicorn status what problems they are solving and what they mean for African technology ecosystems provides valuable insight into the future of African entrepreneurship.

Companies Approaching Unicorn Status#

Kobo360 the Nigerian last mile logistics platform has achieved a valuation exceeding $500 million and is actively fundraising for the Series C round that could push it past the $1 billion unicorn threshold. The company is solving one of Africa’s most pressing logistics challenges connecting individual truck owners with shippers needing freight transported.

Yassir the North African ride hailing platform operating in Tunisia Algeria Morocco and Egypt has raised significant capital and is scaling rapidly with valuations approaching unicorn territory.

Nestia the pan African human resources and talent platform connecting African professionals with remote job opportunities has grown to over $200 million valuation with significant runway to unicorn status if it maintains its growth trajectory.

Splash the Nigerian fashion commerce platform has achieved valuations over $100 million and is expanding to other African countries with a business model of curated fashion content and direct commerce.

What Makes These Companies Special#

The successful African startups approaching unicorn status share common characteristics. They solve genuine acute problems that African users face daily. They have business models that generate real revenue rather than depending on growth at all costs. They have leadership teams with deep knowledge of African markets. They have attracted world class investment capital that validates their potential.

Investment Opportunities#

Several of these near unicorn companies have made equity available to African investors through equity crowdfunding platforms allowing African individuals to participate in the ownership of companies that could create enormous wealth if they achieve unicorn status and eventually exit through acquisition or public listing.

Conclusion#

The emergence of African technology startups approaching unicorn status across diverse sectors signals the maturation of African entrepreneurship and venture capital ecosystems. The companies being built now are not just replicating Silicon Valley ideas adapted for Africa they are building genuinely novel solutions to distinctly African problems that have global relevance and scale potential.
